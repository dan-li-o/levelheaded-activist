About

I study how international policy shapes lived experience—and how careful, data-driven reform can strengthen democratic trust. My work follows the evidence, prioritizes the people most affected, and champions patience over spectacle.

From multilateral ministries to neighborhood coalitions, I collaborate with communities who want reforms that endure across borders. Empathy paired with empirics is how we move from outrage to outcomes.
